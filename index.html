Features

  • It is a two-bedroom, one room has a twin and full-size beds with a cot if it is needed, the second room has queen and twin. There is a futon in the living room that folds out into an extra bed.

  • Downstairs there's a kitchen, living room, and bathroom with a shower. The kitchen currently has a gas oven that will be replaced in the spring. There are two decks downstairs and a small one upstairs.

  • The camp has access to Atv and snowmobile trails. There are kayaks and a canoe available for campers to use.

Pricing

  • $125 per night with a two-night minimum and a non-refundable 125$ deposit is required to hold dates for the camp.

  • Clean the camp before you leave, pets are allowed. A simple, common-sense policy. Firewood is not included in the price.

  • Camp is available between Memorial Day weekend and the end of November.

History

  • Camp History by Owner Michelle Patterson

    My father, Dallas, built the camp back when I was in high school, and I’ve been going there for almost 30 years now. He used lumber from the land and hardware stores. We dug a well since he built it, so our water is excellent and safe to drink and cook with. We used to heat the place with a propane heater and gas lamps. We no longer use those as we have the heat pump, which does a fantastic job of heating and cooling the camp off. I am asking that folks try to bring their own wood as I am not allowed to run a chainsaw.

  • The land is entirely private as there is just one person who lives across the stream, and she is an older lady in her mid 90’s. She loves to see people at the camp. Especially men, she enjoys watching from her house. She’s harmless. We try to be respectful as we ask renters to be respectful of her. She’s a lovely lady.
